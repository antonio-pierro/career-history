In this presentation I will talk about my research activities in the field of high energy physics, in particular in the field of the grid computing.

Motivation

  • It is extremely important for students and external people to find their destination such as laboratories in a large complex buildings, such as a university.
  • A successful implementation of the proposed system would provide visitors of university with their current locations inside the building and the shortest route when searching for a laboratory of interest.

State of the art and limits

  • Google Maps for Android began introducing floor plans of shopping malls, airports, and other large commercial areas.
  • It knows where the WiFi hotspots are in a given building, and through signal strength triangulation it can roughly define where you are.
  • Limits:
    • it provides just 2D navigation
    • in spaces where there are different materials the signal is too noisy.

Challenges

  • The navigation in the building is complicated by:
    • absence of GPS
    • the existence of multiple floors and walls.

Solution

  • Modern smart-phones have several sensors which are pertinent to the localization problem.
  • Such sensors can be:
    • Wifi
    • Bluetooth
    • accelerometer
    • magnetometer
    • gyroscope sensors

Technology adoption

  • PhoneGap to create a user interface using the web technologies.
  • Accelerometer sensor to captures device motion in the x, y, and z direction.
  • Supported platforms: Android, BlackBerry, iPhone.
  • Supported devices: all devices having the "Accelerometer and gyroscope" sensor.
